THE BRIEF HISTORY OF WOMEN IN IRAN

Iran has a long history of women’s activism under different

political groups’ rule. Women have worked for change for over

a century and fought for their freedom and fundamental rights.

The women’s social movement began during the constitutional

revolution in early 1905. And they have struggled with the

patriarchal system for all these years.

Still, the law does not treat them as equal citizens. Examples of

gender discrimination include but are not limited to individual freedom to travel and work, the right to education, and personal status like marriage, divorce, and inheritance.

Here we are talking about contemporary

history of women in Iran

The emergence of a women’s uprising began when significant socio-economic changes were

happening. With the influence of European advisors and diplomats also came European ideas and lifestyles, which awakened many educated men and women to the repressive conditions

of Iranian politics. Women organized street riots, participated in some fights, joined

underground activities against foreign forces, boycotted the import of foreign goods,

participated in the demolition of a Russian bank, and raised funds for the establishment

of the National Bank.
